Project Idea

Game builder for kids. Like the popular game Minecraft that many kids (and adults) love to play, building your own world and exploring different themes and levels made by others or something like Rollercoaster Tycoon, building your own rides and amusement park, the addiction to building things is unreal and my idea is to make a game directed at younger kids that is very user friendly and easy to use, revolving around building your own world.

The game will consist of many pre-designed map tiles consisting of grasslands, ponds, lakes, beaches, deserts and characters, trees, obstacles, fences that the player can select and input into their very own map, designing their world from the ground up. It will be a basic game aimed mainly at mobile but could also be scalable to a much more complex version for older audiences. The game will look similar to the old Pokemon games or Stardew Valley, allowing seamless gameplay on less powerful devices that people may be playing on.

Players will be able to create a game similar to a farming simulator, or completely opposite they could make a shoot’em’up, all dependent on their choices of map tiles and enemy or friendly NPC’s. The game will require some sort of currency as a reward for playing, offering unlocks and upgrades to keep a players attention and motivation for playing alive.

The game will require a lot of skills I do not have currently, such as Unity, Construct2 and others for mobile development. It will also require a lot of graphic design to make the game attractive and lively. It should require no more than a basic PC to create (and play) and a mobile device for testing purposes.

The purpose of this project is not to solve any real world issues or introduce any fancy new programs or algorithms into the world, it’s just a simple game that I believe I would have found really fun when I was growing up, all while introducing the young players into the world of game development and perhaps sparking an interest in IT for them too.
